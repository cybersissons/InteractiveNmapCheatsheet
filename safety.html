SAFETY GUIDE

Essential safety guidelines, legal considerations, and troubleshooting tips for responsible network scanning and security assessment.

⚠️

Legal Notice

Network scanning can be illegal without proper authorization. Always ensure you have explicit written permission to scan networks and systems you don't own. Unauthorized scanning may violate computer fraud laws and result in criminal charges.

Safety Checklist

Best Practices

•
Start with Discovery
Begin with host discovery before port scanning to minimize impact
•
Use Appropriate Timing
Choose timing templates based on network stability and requirements
•
Limit Port Ranges
Scan only necessary ports instead of full 1-65535 range
•
Monitor Network Impact
Watch for performance degradation during scans
•
Document Everything
Keep detailed logs of scanning activities and results

Troubleshooting Guide

Permission Denied Errors

Cannot perform SYN scans or raw socket operations

▼

Problem

TCP SYN scans (-sS) and other raw socket operations require root/administrator privileges. Without proper permissions, you'll get "Permission denied" errors.

Solutions

Run with sudo (Linux/Mac):
sudo nmap -sS target.com
Use TCP connect scan instead:
nmap -sT target.com
This doesn't require root privileges but is more detectable
Run as Administrator (Windows):
Right-click Command Prompt → "Run as administrator"

Slow or Hanging Scans

Scans taking too long or appearing to freeze

▼

Problem

Network scans can be slow due to timeouts, firewalls, or network congestion. UDP scans are particularly slow due to the nature of the protocol.

Solutions

Use faster timing template:
nmap -T4 target.com
Set host timeout:
nmap --host-timeout 30m target.com
Reduce retry attempts:
nmap --max-retries 2 target.com
Skip host discovery:
nmap -Pn target.com
Useful when you know the host is up

No Results or All Ports Filtered

Scan shows no open ports or everything appears filtered

▼

Problem

Firewalls or intrusion prevention systems may be blocking or filtering scan traffic. This can result in all ports appearing filtered or closed.

Solutions

Try different scan types:
nmap -sT target.com
TCP connect scan may bypass some filters
Use ACK scan to detect firewall rules:
nmap -sA target.com
Try fragmenting packets:
nmap -f target.com
Use decoy scanning:
nmap -D RND:10 target.com
Hides scan among decoy traffic

Service Detection Failures

Services not identified or incorrect version detection

▼

Problem

Service version detection may fail due to firewalls, non-standard service configurations, or services that don't respond to standard probes.

Solutions

Increase version detection intensity:
nmap -sV --version-intensity 9 target.com
Try all version probes:
nmap -sV --version-all target.com
Use lighter version detection:
nmap -sV --version-light target.com
Faster but less comprehensive
Combine with NSE scripts:
nmap -sV --script banner target.com

Legal Framework

📋

Authorization

Always obtain explicit written permission before scanning any network or system you don't own. Verbal permission is not sufficient for legal protection.

🎯

Scope Definition

Clearly define scanning scope, methods, and timing in your authorization agreement. Stick to the agreed-upon parameters.

📊

Documentation

Maintain detailed logs of all scanning activities, including dates, times, methods used, and results obtained.
